Understanding PSI and Bar — A Trade Reference

PSI (pounds per square inch) is the standard pressure unit in North America. You'll see it on air compressors, hydraulic equipment, tire gauges, water pressure tests and concrete specifications. One PSI is the pressure exerted by one pound-force over one square inch of area.

Bar is a metric unit equal to exactly 100,000 Pascals (100 kPa). It's used in European equipment specifications, international engineering standards, and is the preferred unit for hydraulic systems in metric countries. One bar is very close to normal atmospheric pressure (1 atm = 1.01325 bar).

When working on projects with international specifications or importing European equipment, you'll need to convert between PSI and bar regularly. The exact conversion factor is 1 PSI = 0.0689476 bar, or equivalently, 1 bar = 14.5038 PSI.

How to Convert PSI to Bar — Step by Step

  1. Identify your PSI value from the gauge, specification sheet, or code requirement.
  2. Multiply by 0.0689476 to get bar. This is the exact SI-defined conversion factor.
  3. Verify the result: bar values are always smaller than PSI values by about a factor of 14.5. If your bar value is larger, you've divided instead of multiplied.

To go the other direction (bar to PSI), multiply the bar value by 14.5038.

Quick mental math: A useful approximation is 1 bar ≈ 15 PSI. So 6 bar ≈ 90 PSI. For rough field estimates, multiply bar by 15 or divide PSI by 15.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you convert PSI to bar? ▶

Multiply the PSI value by 0.0689476 to get bar. For example, 100 PSI × 0.0689476 = 6.895 bar. The reverse — bar to PSI — is multiplying by 14.5038.

What is the difference between PSI and bar? ▶

PSI (pounds per square inch) is an imperial unit common in North America for plumbing, pneumatics, and tire pressure. Bar is a metric unit equal to 100,000 Pascals, used internationally. 1 bar = 14.5038 PSI. Bar is closer to standard atmospheric pressure (1 atm = 1.01325 bar).

What PSI is normal for residential water supply? ▶

Normal residential water pressure runs 40–80 PSI (2.76–5.52 bar). The ideal is around 60 PSI (4.14 bar). Pressure exceeding 80 PSI can damage fixtures and appliances and typically requires a pressure reducing valve (PRV) under UPC code §608.

What air compressor pressure for a framing nailer? ▶

Framing nailers typically operate at 100–120 PSI (6.89–8.27 bar). Most manufacturers recommend 110 PSI (7.58 bar) for optimal drive depth. Always check your nailer's spec plate. Over-pressure can cause misfires, double-drives, and seal damage.

What is the compressive strength of concrete in PSI and MPa? ▶

Normal structural concrete has a 28-day compressive strength (f'c) of 3,000–4,000 PSI (20.7–27.6 MPa). High-strength concrete for bridges and high-rise columns reaches 5,000–10,000 PSI (34.5–69 MPa). Residential slabs typically use 3,000 PSI (20.7 MPa) minimum per ACI 318.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many mm in an inch?▶

Exactly 25.4 mm per inch. This is the SI-defined exact conversion (1 inch = 25.4 mm by international agreement since 1959). To convert inches to mm, multiply by 25.4. To convert mm to inches, divide by 25.4 or multiply by 0.03937.

Why is a 2×4 not actually 2 inches by 4 inches?▶

Nominal lumber dimensions refer to the green (unseasoned) rough-cut size. After drying and surfacing (S4S — surfaced four sides), lumber shrinks. A 2×4 is 1.5" × 3.5" after surfacing. This standard has been consistent since the 1960s per NIST PS 20.

To convert watts to amps, divide watts by volts: Amps = Watts ÷ Volts (Ohm's Law). For a 2,400W load on a 240V circuit: 2400 ÷ 240 = 10 amps. For continuous loads, apply the 80% NEC derating: 10A ÷ 0.8 = 12.5A minimum breaker (use 15A).

What is the 80% rule for circuit breakers?▶

NEC 210.20(A) requires that continuous loads (on for 3+ hours) not exceed 80% of a breaker's rated ampacity. A 20A breaker can only supply 16A continuously. For sizing: divide your continuous load amps by 0.8 to find the minimum breaker. Example: 16A load ÷ 0.8 = 20A breaker minimum.

How do I convert AWG to mm²?▶

AWG to mm² doesn't have a simple multiplication formula — use the reference table above. Common equivalents: 14 AWG ≈ 2.5 mm², 12 AWG ≈ 4 mm², 10 AWG ≈ 6 mm², 8 AWG ≈ 10 mm², 6 AWG ≈ 16 mm². These are the nearest standard IEC cross-sections used in European specifications.
