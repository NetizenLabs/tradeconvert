What Water Pressure Is Too High? A Plumber's Guide to PSI Limits

Residential water pressure above 80 PSI silently destroys fixtures, shortens appliance life, and violates plumbing code. Here's how to test it and fix it.

TradeConvert Editorial Published Jan 15, 2025 ⏱ 7 min read

Water pressure above 80 PSI (5.52 bar) is too high for residential plumbing. The Uniform Plumbing Code (UPC §608) requires a pressure reducing valve when street pressure exceeds this limit. Ideal residential pressure is 40–60 PSI (2.76–4.14 bar).

Why Water Pressure Matters

Most homeowners treat water pressure as a set-and-forget system detail. It's not. High pressure is one of the most common causes of premature plumbing failure. The damage happens slowly and invisibly until a pipe bursts or a fixture starts leaking.

"Most fixture manufacturers explicitly void warranties when operating pressure exceeds 80 PSI."

How to Test Your Pressure

  1. 1
    Attach a gauge to an outdoor spigot.
  2. 2
    Turn off all water inside the house.
  3. 3
    Open the spigot valve fully and read the needle.
⚠ Warning If your pressure is above 80 PSI, you need a Pressure Reducing Valve (PRV) immediately to protect your home.
